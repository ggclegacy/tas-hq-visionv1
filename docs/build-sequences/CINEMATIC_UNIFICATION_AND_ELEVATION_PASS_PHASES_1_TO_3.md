## Final cinematic language

The creative bar is a fusion of luxury automotive reveal, premium medical architecture, aerospace precision, Apple restraint, masculine executive confidence, and advanced institutional intelligence.

### Living world

Build one persistent cinematic environment that evolves across the Prologue, Act I, and Act II. It must contain authored, bounded movement even during still moments:

- obsidian architectural volume rather than a flat background;
- machined graphite and restrained dark glass;
- layered foreground, midground, and background planes;
- subtle volumetric-looking beams or CSS/SVG equivalents;
- controlled apothecary-green intelligence light;
- moving reflections and light travel;
- selective atmospheric depth, haze, occlusion, and parallax;
- champagne/machined-gold highlights with dark bronze only in shadow;
- warm ivory typography;
- continuous camera intent and changing spatial relationships.

No random visual system is required. Deterministic bounded motion tied to canonical time is preferred.

### Color contract

Create named, reusable luxury tokens and remove ad hoc muddy values from production styling.

- **Obsidian:** neutral-to-green black with real shadow separation; never a crushed flat void.
- **Graphite:** cool machined structure with legible edges.
- **Dark glass:** restrained transmissive-looking surfaces without glassmorphism cards.
- **Apothecary green:** deep, controlled intelligence light; not neon and not a full-screen green wash.
- **Champagne gold:** clean warm metallic highlight; bright enough to read as refined metal, not mustard.
- **Machined gold:** slightly deeper structural gold for edges and precision locks.
- **Bronze:** shadow/accent only; never the primary gold text color.
- **Warm ivory:** primary readable type; never stark browser white.

Metallic dimensionality must come from a controlled highlight/midtone/shadow range, moving light, contrast, and material context—not from turning all text yellow.

### Typography contract

- Keep approved words and meaning.
- Use warm ivory as the default; reserve dimensional gold for selective emphasis.
- Reveal complete semantic phrases cleanly; do not strand half a sentence while waiting for the second half.
- Typical primary copy reveal: `450–900ms`.
- Typical secondary copy reveal: `350–700ms`.
- Multi-line copy may cascade within `80–160ms` per line only when all lines settle within the total reveal budget.
- Reading holds begin when the complete thought is legible, not when the first fragment appears.
- Prefer off-axis framing, thirds, negative space, masking, depth, and light-motivated reveals over centered website stacks.
- Do not overuse letter spacing, ultra-thin weights, generic SaaS heading/paragraph arrangements, or typewriter effects.

### Camera and composition contract

- Every shot defines emotional purpose, focal subject, framing, camera state, depth layers, lighting, continuous movement, copy region, and outgoing transition.
- Camera movement must be authored from canonical time and remain seekable.
- Favor controlled dolly, lateral tracking, macro-to-wide reveals, focal reorientation, layered parallax, foreground passes, and restrained push-ins.
- Avoid arbitrary orbiting, constant floating, spring motion, bouncing, repeated scale-ups, and rotation without narrative purpose.
- Do not center everything. Use close detail, wide architecture, off-axis subjects, asymmetry, foreground obstruction, and scale contrast.
- Motion must have hierarchy. The subject, camera, environment, copy, and lighting must not all compete at full intensity.

### Cross-scene continuity contract

- No full black/green reset between normal adjacent shots.
- The next shot should begin forming during the prior shot's exit window.
- At least one continuity carrier—camera vector, architecture, light path, reflection, atmosphere, emblem, or foreground plane—must bridge each adjacent shot unless a deliberate hard cut is narratively justified.
- Standard overlap window: `500–1,200ms`; hero transitions may use up to `1,600ms` when motion remains active.
- Crossfades alone are insufficient. Occlusion, camera passage, light change, depth handoff, material transformation, or composition match should motivate transitions.
- A scene may become quiet, but it must never look frozen or finished while the timeline continues.

### Per-beat budgets

- Environmental pickup before meaningful content: normally `0–600ms`; never several empty seconds.
- Logo becomes clearly identifiable: normally within `1,200–2,400ms` of its shot beginning.
- Hero logo activation: approximately `2,000–3,500ms`, with useful motion/light visible immediately.
- Primary text entrance: `450–900ms`.
- Supporting line entrance: `350–700ms`.
- Readable hold after full reveal:
  - short label/title: `1,000–1,800ms`;
  - one concise sentence: `1,800–3,200ms`;
  - two short lines: `2,500–4,000ms` when necessary.
- Transition overlap: normally `500–1,200ms`.
- No unexplained static hold longer than `3,500ms`.
- No shot should use a uniform nine-second template merely because previous manifests did.
- If silence is intentional, visual development must continue.

Use automated timing tests to flag:

- entrances over budget;
