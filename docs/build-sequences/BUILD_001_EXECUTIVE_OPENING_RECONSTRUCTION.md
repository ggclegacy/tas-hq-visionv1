1. Gent Ascend Collective presents;

## 5. Permanent creative requirements for this build

### One living world

Build a persistent, evolving executive intelligence environment—not a flat green gradient or looping wallpaper. Use performant combinations of:

- obsidian and machined graphite architecture;
- dark glass and controlled metallic planes;
- foreground, midground, and background separation;
- architectural framing, occlusion, and parallax;
- deep Apothecary-green intelligence light;
- dimensional champagne-gold reflection and highlights;
- restrained atmosphere, light shafts, reflection drift, and bounded particulate detail;
- environmental changes motivated by narration, camera, and shot transitions.

Avoid neon cyberpunk, blue science-fiction HUDs, generic grids, excessive bloom, constant lens flares, random particles, empty fog, glassmorphism cards, pharmacy clichés, and decorative motion without narrative purpose.

### Premium logo artifacts

Treat both logos as physical hero artifacts within the world. The reusable choreography may include bounded axial response, subtle rotation, breathing, internal-looking illumination, rim lighting, specular sweeps, shadow, reflection, parallax, controlled pulse, foreground interaction, and camera-relative entrance/exit.

The GAC logo must feel confident and refined. The TAS HQ emblem must be larger, more commanding, and become the spatial gateway into Act I. Neither logo may simply fade in, sit still, and fade out. Avoid fast spinning, hover-widget pulsing, distortion, and generic glow filters.

### Camera and transitions

The camera must appear to travel continuously through the same world. Use controlled push, track, focal reorientation, macro-to-wide changes, foreground passage, architectural parallax, light travel, masking, occlusion, or material transformation.

Every adjacent shot requires an explicit continuity carrier. The next visual idea should begin forming before the previous one fully disappears. A crossfade alone is not a transition. Do not reset to an empty background between ideas.

### Typography and copy

Use warm ivory for primary reading copy and dimensional champagne gold only for significance. Eliminate muddy yellow-brown, mustard, flat tan, and orange-gold from the opening production path.

Text must be deliberately composed for the shot. Center hero identity and dedication when appropriate; use thirds, negative space, asymmetry, and scale contrast elsewhere. Do not put every line at bottom-left. Do not use website-style eyebrow/heading/paragraph stacks.

Reveal complete semantic phrases. Never reveal half a sentence, wait, and then reveal the rest. Primary entrances should generally complete in 450–850ms, secondary text in 300–650ms, with reading holds generally near 1.2–2.2 seconds after full legibility.

Rewrite weak opening language to be specific, respectful, and human. The story should honor:

- trust earned through patient care;
- knowledge behind confident guidance;
- standards carried by people;
- leadership protecting consistency;
- growth without losing what makes The Apothecary Shoppe exceptional.

Avoid “clarity, stewardship, continuity,” generic transformation slogans, consulting filler, unsupported superlatives, product features, and paragraphs. Preserve the approved dedication. Onyx narration should carry most exposition. If final audio is absent, do not synthesize a substitute and do not use browser text-to-speech; implement exact captions and deterministic replaceable narration hooks.

### Responsive and performance requirements

Author intentional compositions for mobile portrait, tablet, desktop, and executive display. Do not merely shrink desktop framing. Preserve subject priority, logo scale, readable type, safe captions, controls, and cinematic depth at every viewport.

Neil develops on an older Mac. Prefer efficient DOM/SVG/CSS/2.5D layering and selective real-time effects. Add WebGL only if repository evidence shows that it materially improves a signature moment and includes a premium non-WebGL fallback. Bound blur, composited layers, particles, geometry, draw calls, shaders, pixel ratio, post-processing, and perpetual work. Only active visual work should consume resources.

Preserve meaningful low/balanced/enhanced quality tiers and reduced motion. Lower tiers simplify effects, never story, composition, logo dignity, or typography. Report only performance actually measured; never invent FPS results.
