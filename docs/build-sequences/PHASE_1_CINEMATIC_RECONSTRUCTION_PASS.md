## Non-negotiable creative contract

The opening is one directed journey:

`living environment → motivated reveal → camera travel → overlapping transformation → new focal subject within the same world`

Never return to:

`blank background → line of text → long hold → fade out → next line`

Aim for roughly 70% cinematic visual storytelling, 20% brand/system revelation, and no more than 10% large title copy. Build approximately **five to seven authored shots**, not one scene per narration sentence. Limit frame-dominating title moments to:

1. Gent Ascend Collective presents;
2. the exclusive dedication;
3. TAS HQ / the Act I handoff.

Supporting words may appear briefly inside visual sequences but must not create more isolated title cards.

### Living environment

Replace the flat green background with a persistent, evolving world using layered architectural geometry; foreground, midground, and background separation; machined graphite; dark glass planes; moving light shafts or efficient volumetric-looking equivalents; subtle atmosphere; deterministic parallax; foreground occlusion; apothecary-green intelligence-light travel; champagne-gold reflections; and continuous bounded environmental life.

The world must evolve with each shot rather than loop as animated wallpaper. Avoid cyberpunk neon, blue HUDs, generic grids, random particle noise, excessive bloom, constant lens flares, glassmorphism cards, and pharmacy clichés.

### Luxury color system

Create/consolidate named tokens for:

- deep obsidian with real shadow separation;
- cool machined graphite;
- restrained dark glass;
- refined deep apothecary green;
- bright dimensional champagne gold;
- deeper machined gold for structure;
- bronze for shadow only;
- warm ivory typography.

Eliminate muddy brown, mustard, orange-yellow, and flat tan from the opening production path. Gold must read through highlight/midtone/shadow, contrast, and moving light—not by making large text yellow.

### Composition

Recompose every shot deliberately. Center logos when they are the hero focal point. Use thirds, asymmetry, negative space, foreground obstruction, and scale contrast where the shot demands them. Never default all narrative text to the bottom-left. Avoid website-style eyebrow/heading/paragraph stacks. Make type feel revealed through light, depth, masking, or camera motion. Author distinct mobile portrait, tablet, desktop, and executive-display framing.

Every shot must define its emotional objective, focal subject, camera/framing, depth layers, lighting, environmental motion, copy region, responsive variants, and outgoing transition carrier.

### Logo artifacts

Create one reusable, manifest-driven logo choreography system for both protected marks, supporting confident responsive scale; subtle axial rotation/perspective; restrained physical breathing; specular sweeps; internal-looking illumination; rim light; halo; reflection; shadow; parallax; foreground interaction; elegant scaling; and deterministic seek/pause/resume/replay/reduced-motion behavior.

The GAC logo establishes authorship. The TAS HQ emblem is the larger, commanding culmination and spatial gateway into Act I. Neither may simply fade in, sit still, and fade out. Avoid fast rotation, hover-style pulses, and distortion.

### Copy and narration

Rewrite the opening to be specific to The Apothecary Shoppe and honor what Blair and Bailey built: trust earned through patient care, knowledge behind confident guidance, standards carried by people, leadership protecting consistency, and growth without losing what makes the organization exceptional.

Avoid vague corporate language such as “clarity, stewardship, continuity,” generic transformation slogans, consulting filler, unsupported claims, feature lists, and paragraphs. Directional examples—not mandatory final wording—include:

- “What Blair and Bailey built lives in the people who carry it.”
- “The knowledge behind every confident answer.”
- “The care behind every patient interaction.”
- “A standard too important to leave to chance.”

Preserve the approved dedication. Onyx narration carries most exposition; on-screen text is short, memorable, and emotionally resonant. If final audio is absent, do not synthesize a substitute. Implement exact captions, silent playback, and replaceable deterministic audio hooks.

### Pacing and continuity

Target Prologue runtime: **35–50 seconds** from Begin through the Act I threshold.

- Start meaningful visual development immediately.
- Make a hero logo recognizable within roughly `1,200–2,400ms` of its reveal.
- Complete primary text entrances in `450–850ms`; secondary copy in `300–650ms`.
- Reveal complete semantic phrases—never half a thought followed by a long wait.
- Keep short reading holds near `1,200–2,200ms` after full legibility.
- Allow no unexplained static hold over `3,000ms`.
- Overlap adjacent shots by roughly `500–1,200ms`.
- Begin the next visual idea before the prior one fully disappears.
- Use camera travel, light, occlusion, focus, reflection, or material/spatial transformation; a crossfade alone is not a transition.

Do not merely speed up the old slideshow. Re-edit visual ideas, narration, logos, camera, and transitions into one flow.

### Reusable systems and runtime preservation

Build/consolidate reusable systems for environment layers, deterministic camera choreography, logo choreography, complete-thought type reveals, lighting cues, transition overlap, quality tiers, reduced motion, viewport profiles, and shot metadata.

Preserve the Presentation Director as the single source of truth, including autoplay, pause/resume, seek, replay, chapter behavior, captions, timing infrastructure, visibility recovery, and runtime architecture. Do not create component-owned clocks, scattered `setTimeout` chains, or animation callbacks that advance story state.

### Older-Mac performance

Use selective WebGL only where it materially improves a signature moment. Prefer performant CSS/SVG/DOM/2.5D layering elsewhere. Bound geometry, draw calls, shaders, blur, particles, pixel ratio, post-processing, and composited layers. Preload/decode hero logos. Preserve premium low/balanced/enhanced quality behavior, reduced motion, silent fallback, and unsupported-WebGL fallback. Lower tiers simplify effects, not composition or narrative quality. Report only performance actually measured.
