## Act II narrative contract

### Executive purpose

Act II communicates one idea:

> Growth does not weaken a strong standard. It increases the distance that standard must travel.

The act creates respectful tension around five natural consequences of scale:

1. **Knowledge scatters** — expertise lives across more people, conversations, documents, and locations.
2. **Training becomes inconsistent** — the same standard becomes harder to transfer with identical clarity every time.
3. **Leadership repeats information** — important guidance must be delivered again across different moments and audiences.
4. **Communication fragments** — messages move through more channels, layers, and interpretations.
5. **Standards become harder to scale** — what once traveled through proximity must now travel through systems.

These are authorized strategic observations from the original master brief. They must be expressed as universal organizational dynamics, not claims that a specific incident, deficiency, error, complaint, or performance problem exists at The Apothecary Shoppe.

### Emotional progression

**Continuity → Expansion → Distance → Multiplication → Friction → Recognition → Strategic tension → Possibility**

The act should begin inside the confident world created by The Standard. That world expands. Paths multiply. Information travels farther. Small differences in timing, distance, and interpretation become visible. Nothing breaks or collapses. By the end, the audience should feel: “This is the natural next challenge of protecting something valuable as it grows.”
