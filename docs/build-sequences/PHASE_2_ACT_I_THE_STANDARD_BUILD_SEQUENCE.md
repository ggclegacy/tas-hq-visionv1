## Act I narrative contract

### Executive purpose

Act I communicates one idea:

> The Apothecary Shoppe already possesses a standard worth preserving and carrying forward.

It honors five qualities:

1. **Trust** — earned through consistency, responsibility, and human care.
2. **Knowledge** — expertise that lives in people, judgment, practice, and shared understanding.
3. **Culture** — the way the organization behaves when no instruction is being read.
4. **People** — the individuals who carry the standard into every interaction.
5. **Leadership** — the stewardship that gives the standard clarity and continuity.

Act I must not introduce TAS HQ as software, show application interfaces, describe features, state a challenge, criticize current operations, or imply that technology created the organization's value.

### Emotional progression

**Threshold → Recognition → Human weight → Institutional confidence → Stewardship → Collective synthesis → Forward tension**

The intended emotional result is quiet pride and recognition, followed by curiosity about how something valuable can be carried forward as the organization grows.

### Approved on-screen language

The following language is authorized. Codex may adjust line breaks, capitalization, punctuation, reveal order, and whether a supporting line appears visually or only in accessible text, but it must not invent factual claims, metrics, awards, quotes, patient outcomes, or historical events.

- `Act I`
- `The Standard`
- `Some organizations are built.`
- `Others are carried.`
- `Trust`
- `Earned in every interaction.`
- `Knowledge`
- `Made valuable when it is shared.`
- `Culture`
- `The standard people carry when no one is watching.`
- `People`
- `The human system behind every experience.`
- `Leadership`
- `Clarity. Stewardship. Continuity.`
- `What has been built here is more than a business.`
- `It is a standard.`
- `Act II`

Use this copy with restraint. Not every line must occupy its own shot. The act must not feel like a slideshow of single words.
